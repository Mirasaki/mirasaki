My developer journey this far

I started my self-taught development journey about 2 years ago, back in 2020. Initially, I just wanted to make Discord bots that me and my friends were interested in. We were looking for specific functionality that wasn't publicly available at the time and so I thought to myself Why not just make it myself?

And so I started my journey. I kicked things off with The Odin Project's Foundations course but gave up and stopped following the course about 2 weeks in. I just didn't enjoy it. I knew I was supposed to be learning the foundations and basics of development, no matter what specific area. But I just wanted to make Discord bots. So I stopped the course, and instead went to YouTube and looked for some JavaScript/Node videos on how to create those Discord bots that I wanted to make so badly.

I found some tutorials by a YouTube channel called Worn Off Keys and quickly fell in love with creating Discord bots that I could show off to my friends. Although I was proud of what I was creating, I was struggling with the core concept of Node/JavaScript and how to use it. I had to google every simple task and operation. I learned how to effectively use search engines to get the answer I was looking for, but I didn't properly understand the answers I was given. I did realize this. I was struggling like this for about six months, the first half-year of my developer journey. I was just incredibly proud of the small things I did know how to do and wanted to build more, that's why I kept going like this, for way longer than I should have.

But eventually, I did realize something had to change in order for me to be efficient in any way, shape or form. I started by going to the MDN JavaScript documentation and taking courses on Udemy, learning all the basics from working with strings, data, mapping, objects, and loops - to understanding scopes, all the different available data structures, working with APIs, memory management, event-loop based concurrency, synchronous and asynchronous JS and the differences between them.

So that's what I've been doing this far - almost 2 years in - working with NodeJS (non-browser JavaScript) and API's. Pretty much doing what I wanted to do. This all started out of interest, a hobby. And I thoroughly enjoyed every single day of it except maybe for that first half year of not having the slightest clue as to what I was doing.

And now here we are, May 2022, and I just started a web development boot-camp on Udemy. I've made a couple of mediocre, unimpressive sites this far, and I am eager to learn more and some day, hopefully, realizing my dream of becoming a full-stack developer, as there is nothing I'd rather do.
